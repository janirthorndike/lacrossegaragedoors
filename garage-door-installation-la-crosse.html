A New Garage Door for Your La Crosse Home

Whether you're replacing a tired, dented door or picking one for new construction, we'll help you choose a door that fits, holds up to Wisconsin winters, and looks right on your house — then give you a clear quote. Tell us a bit about it below.

Repairs are the heart of what we do, but a door past saving — or a brand-new build — deserves the right replacement. The two things that matter most in this climate are insulation and build quality. An insulated steel door keeps an attached garage warmer, runs quieter, and stands up to years of freeze-and-thaw better than a thin single-layer door. The style matters too: the right door lifts a home's whole front elevation, whether that's clean flush panels, a carriage-house look, or panels with a row of windows for daylight.

Tell us your door's size, how you use the garage, and the look you're after. We'll come back with options and an honest quote — no pressure, and no upselling you into more door than you need.

Good to know

Do new doors here need to be insulated?

For an attached or heated garage, insulation is worth it in the La Crosse climate — an insulated steel door holds temperature far better through winter, runs quieter, and lasts longer. For a detached, unheated garage it matters less. We'll help you weigh it.

Can you reuse my existing opener?

Usually, yes, if it's in good shape. If it's older or underpowered for a heavier insulated door, we'll tell you straight and leave the choice to you.

How long does installation take?

A standard single or double replacement is typically a one-day job once the door's in. Lead time depends on the door and finish you pick — we'll give you a realistic timeline with the quote.
