Opener repair

Garage Door Opener Repair in La Crosse

Opener humming but the door won't budge? Reversing before it closes? Remote gone dead? We repair all major opener brands across the La Crosse area — and we'll tell you honestly when a repair makes sense versus a replacement.

An opener problem can look like a dozen different things, but most trace back to a handful of causes. The good news: an opener issue is usually cheaper and simpler than it feels, and it's rarely the door itself.

What we repair

  • Won't respond to the remote or keypad — often a dead battery, a wiring issue, or a unit that needs reprogramming.
  • Motor runs but the door doesn't move — a disengaged trolley or a stripped drive gear, not the door.
  • Door reverses before closing — misaligned or dirty safety sensors near the floor.
  • Grinding, straining, or loud operation — worn gears, a failing motor, or a door that's out of balance and overworking the opener.
  • Nothing at all — power, logic board, or capacitor issues.

All major brands

We service LiftMaster, Chamberlain, Genie, Craftsman, Sommer, and the rest. If you're not sure what you've got, we'll identify it and bring compatible parts — brand compatibility is part of what we handle so you don't have to.

Repair or replace?

Not every opener is worth fixing, and we won't pretend otherwise. If yours is newer and the fault is a sensor, gear, or remote, a repair is the obvious call. If it's decades old, underpowered for your door, and the motor's going, we'll lay out the honest math on replacing it — and never push you toward the bigger ticket without a real reason.

A door that's hard to lift by hand will wear out any opener fast. If yours strains, the fix may actually be the springs or balance, not the opener. We check both.

Common questions

Motor runs but the door won't move?

Usually the trolley disconnected from the carriage (someone pulled the red release cord) or the drive gear stripped. Both are quick to diagnose and far cheaper than a new opener.

Door reverses right before it closes?

That's the floor-level safety sensors — misaligned, dirty, or blocked. Make sure both lights are solid, wipe the lenses, and clear the path. If it still reverses, the sensors or wiring need a look.
